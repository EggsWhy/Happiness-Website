Getting insights from the Data

So as you can obviously see here, the happiest country is Finland. Almost all these countries are European countries with the exception of Israel and New Zealand. Best tip is to just go study Finnish and move to Finland. Now you might wonder why all these countries have such high happiness scores. What makes a country happier? This is exactly what we will be looking at in the next part.

Here are 4 different factors that affect the Happiness of a country. For both GDP per capita and Healthy Life Expectancy, we can see that it has a postive relationship with the happiness score. As one increases the other also increases. You can also see that there is an even distrubtion of GDP per capita and Healthy Life Expectancy. Theres countries with higher and countries with lower GDP per capita and Healthy Life Expectancy.

However for the graph showing the countries' freedom you can see that a majority of the points are on the right side of the graph this means that majority of countries give their people freedom to do whatever they want.

You could have probably guessed this. When the people of a country view their country as corrupt, they will definetly not feel very happy and will decrease their happiness score. You can also see that a majority of the points on the graph are on the right hand side which suggests that the people of most countries view their country as corrupt

A bonus factor. As you can see there is barely any correlation between the generosity of a country in relation to their happiness score. I didn't even know you can receive a negative generosity score. With a majority of the data points of the left hand side, we can conclude that most countries are heartless not very nice.
